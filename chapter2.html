Chapter 2

A Brief History of Deep Learning

Deep Learning is currently powering some of the most powerful AIs. We are going to give you a sense of how Deep Learning has come to where it is today.

Scroll

To understand how deep learning has progressed, we may first look at its inspiration: the neuron. While exploring this question, we may get a glimpse of how AI extracts valuable sensory experiences from this chaotic world and solidify them into algorithms that are “smart”.

A neuron is the building block of the human brains. Neurons are nervous cells that take in some stimuli from other neurons, and when enough stimuli are received, it fires a signal (stimulus) to other connected neurons.

Inspired by neurons, computer scientists create perceptrons that take in many stimuli, or inputs, and outputs one value. We will go more in-depth about perceptrons in the next chapter.

It turns out that perceptrons are far from enough to learn complicated concepts. Simpler concepts, such as logical AND, can be learned by a perceptron. The model can find a way to cut a line in the space so that it can correctly produce the result of a logical and, as shown in the animation.

More complex concepts, such as XOR, cannot be learned by a simple perceptron. This is due to the “non-linearity” of the concept, or in simple terms, we cannot find a straight line to divide the circle in two homogeneous groups.

To solve this problem, computer scientists turn to more complicated “simulations” of the brain. A Feed-forward Neural Network is a network of perceptrons that are organized into layers. In theory, these simple neural networks, if trained long enough on a good set of data, can learn everything from classifying images to predicting income.

Simple Feed-forward Neural Networks sound very nice, but they have their own problems. Smaller networks bottlenecks quickly on complex problems, while deeper networks need a lot more computation power. This time, computer scientists looked at higher level human learning mechanisms for inspirations.

One such inspiration is that we humans conceptualize fundamental building blocks of things, and combine them to create more complex concepts. We recognize a dog as having a nose, two ears, and four legs. This behavior inspired computer scientists to create Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), which transforms images into more fundamental constructs, such as basic shapes.

The power of Deep Learning made traditional some machine learning techniques went out of fashion. With enough data and computation power, Deep Learning is able to achieve a level of performance traditional algorithms could never hope for. With recent developments in computer hardware, and the abundance of data, we are finally able to apply Deep Learning to the real world.

Summary

Now, that you have a general idea of the inspirations behind some of the most important concepts in Deep Learning, you are prepared to get in to the details of how things work. In the next chapter, we will discuss perceptrons, the idea that is behind all of Deep Learning.

Glossary

Perceptron: An algorithm inspired by neurons. A single layer neural network that performs binary classification on input data.

Feed-forward Neural Networks: The first and simplest type of artificial neural network. Many perceptrons arranged into layers. Can perform more complex tasks than perceptrons.

Convolutional Neural Networks: A type of artificial neural networks that is mainly used to analyzing and classifying images.
